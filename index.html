Nicholas Williams

Since the beginning of September 2022, I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Lancaster University working with David Pauksztello. During August and September this year, I am also a visiting researcher at the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology.

I received my doctorate from the University of Cologne in 2022 under the supervision of Sibylle Schroll. Following that, I was a JSPS short-term postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Tokyo working with Osamu Iyama from late May to late August 2022.

My research focuses on the representation theory of finite-dimensional algebras and combinatorics. In the representation theory of finite-dimensional algebras I am particularly interested in
  • higher homological algebra,
  • maximal green sequences,
  • stability conditions,
  • relations with cluster algebras.
In combinatorics, I am particularly interested in
  • partially ordered sets, especially the higher Bruhat and Stasheff–Tamari orders,
  • polytopes and their triangulations, especially cyclic polytopes.
I am naturally interested in the interplay between these two areas too.
